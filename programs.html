Our Programs & Initiatives

Community Gardens

Community Gardens

We establish and maintain community gardens in low-income areas, providing both fresh produce and educational opportunities. Our gardens serve as:

  • Sources of fresh, nutritious food for the community
  • Educational centers for sustainable agriculture practices
  • Community gathering spaces that foster social connections
  • Training grounds for aspiring urban farmers

To date, we've established 25 community gardens across Johannesburg, each managed by local volunteers with our support.

Nutrition Education

Nutrition Education

Our weekly nutrition seminars teach community members about:

  • The nutritional value of different fruits and vegetables
  • Budget-friendly healthy meal preparation
  • Food preservation techniques to reduce waste
  • Special dietary needs for different age groups

We partner with local schools, community centers, and healthcare facilities to reach the broadest audience possible.

Food Distribution

Our food distribution program ensures that the fresh produce from our gardens reaches those who need it most. This program includes:

  • Weekly vegetable distribution to registered low-income families
  • Emergency food parcels for households in crisis
  • School nutrition programs that provide fresh produce to partnering schools
  • Collaboration with local food banks to maximize our reach
